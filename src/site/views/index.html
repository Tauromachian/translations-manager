The problem

Why Translation workflows break

Standard approaches to i18n create friction at every level — for coders, translators, and managers alike.

  1. They are difficult to re-use.
  2. It is difficult to separate the development experience from the translators experience.
  3. Pipelines cost money. Generally you don't want to rebuilt your entire project for every change to the translations.

Why this exists?

Built out of frustration

As a developer, spending time on translations — only to have them overwritten — prompted this tool. The goal: make everyone happy.

  • Managers can edit the translations themselves if they choose to.
  • You can hire translators.
  • You can spare your coders of writing translations themselves. Keep them doing what they like. Building software.
  • You can save money. By keeping your coders coding.
